Australian Games Industry

March 25, 2017

The State of the Games Industry

The influx of indie developers around the world continues to grow year by year as new technologies continue to be released and improved. It was just a few years ago that AAA games dominated the market, but now with game engines such as Unity and Unreal Engine becoming free to use [1] for the whole community, the barrier of entry to game development is as low as ever.

Not only are development tools becoming free and easy to use, it is also becoming increasingly easier to distribute games to the community. Steam, one of the main storefronts with over 125 million active users released Steam Greenlight in 2012 which allowed all developers to upload their games and have their submissions be accepted into the Steam Store after going through a voting process. Smaller distribution companies also started to emerge, such as the Humble Store with it's popular Humble Bundles to support charities and small game developers with a pay-what-you-want model.

With that, many new indie game studios have emerged and are popping up to take advantage of the slow release cycle of major games and showcase their own games to the world! Now is the best time to get started, but how does one get started in a field this big? Where do you go to, what resources are there to utilize? There are many opportunities in Australia to kickstart your game development career, and here are just a few of them!

The Australian GameDev Revolution

There are many different opportunities in Australia to start your own small/indie game development company! Firstly, in order to actually produce a game, you need a place to work. What other place is better than Melbourne's own collaborate studio, The Arcade.

The Arcade

Screencap of The Arcade from Game development in Melbourne featuring The Arcade, EA Firemonkeys, Loveshack and GEELab.

The Arcade, located at 71-75 City Rd, Southbank, Victoria 3006 is a collaborative working space which houses many small indie game-dev companies all in one building. The rooms can be rented out for days (or months) at a rate of $28.60/day or $434.72/month according to The Loop. Not only is this an inexpensive way of having a central location for all your developers to work, it can also be an amazing way of getting feedback for games you're working on and inspiration from others working on similar projects.

One of the prime examples of developer success is Crossy Road, a video game created by founders Matt Hall and Andy Sum of Hipster Whale. Originally intended for mobile devices, it is now played across the world by children and in arcades. The Arcade was Hipster Whale's main work location, and goes to show that this environment can house Australia's next best developers.

"You might be a one or two-person team, but when you're part of The Arcade it's like you've got dozens of helpers ready to assist with their expertise." - Ben Britten

Film Victoria

Having a space to work in is great, but space isn't the only thing new game-dev companies require. Having enough funds is just as important as having a space to work in, which is why Film Victoria released their Games Funding initiative to help support game developers produce and release games to the public.

Film Victoria allows game developers to apply for two grants: the Assigned Production Investment grant and the Games Release grant.

The Assigned Production Investment for games grant was created to allow game-dev companies to produce prototypes and full games and also to handle some related activities; including marketing. The grant requires you to pay the non-refundable fee of $550 AUD initially, but afterwards allows your company to receive up to $150,000 AUD (depending on the game itself) to help kickstart your game development so you don't have to focus on acquiring cash/funding.

The Games Release grant on the other hand is for games which are nearing completion. If received, the grant provides up to $30,000 AUD for release related activites such as legal fees, marketing, licensing costs etc.

These grants allow anyone to start working on games which is what Film Victoria is pushing for, and is why Australia is a great place to start creating games.

PAX Australia

Image from Kotaku Australia, Australian Indies At PAX Are Keeping Local Multiplayer Alive

Once you've finished creating your game, you need to showcase it for the world to see! PAX Australia, aka. the Penny Arcade Expo is an amazing event where many new and existing game developers come and showcase their games in their own boothes for the community to see. PAX occurs once a year for each 'region' (Aus, West/South/East), and it is the perfect time to measure how well your game does in terms of playability and work out how much you need to improve in terms of developing the game itself, and marketing.

Opportunities, opportunities

There are so many resources that you can utilize as a game developer in Australia to kickstart your game development company or journey! Looking for a place where you can work or garner opinions? The Arcade is a great place to start with it's collaborative environment and cheap desk spaces. Looking for money to fund your business or yourself? Film Victoria will help you with two separate grants to assist in developing the game itself or releasing a game. Finally, need to quickly and effectively market your game? PAX Australia gathers many passionate gamers who want to explore new and interesting games, showcasing your game in a booth in PAX is definitely the best way to reveal it to the world.
